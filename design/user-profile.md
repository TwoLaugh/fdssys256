# User Profile

## Everything the System Needs to Know About You

### Identity & Household
- Name
- Household size (who are you cooking for?)
- Per-person overrides (e.g., "partner is lactose intolerant but I'm not")
  — even in single-user mode, you might cook for others with different needs

### Hard Constraints (never violate)
- **Allergies**: nuts, shellfish, gluten, soy, eggs, dairy, sesame, etc.
  - Severity level? (anaphylaxis vs mild reaction) — probably treat all as absolute for safety
- **Dietary identity**: vegan, vegetarian, pescatarian, halal, kosher, none
- **Medical diets**: low histamine, low FODMAP, diabetic-friendly, renal diet, etc.

### Soft Constraints (strong preference, but flexible)
- **Intolerances**: lactose, fructose, etc. — "avoid usually, but I'll have cheese occasionally"
- **Dislikes**: specific ingredients (coriander, olives, anchovies)
- **Cuisine dislikes**: "I don't like most traditional Japanese food" (rare but exists)

### Nutrition Goals
- Daily calorie target (or range, e.g., 2000-2200)
- Macro targets: protein (g), carbs (g), fat (g)
  - Or ratios: 40/30/30
  - Or just a priority: "high protein" without exact numbers
- Micro targets? (iron, vitamin D, fibre) — probably v2, adds complexity
- Goal context: bulking, cutting, maintenance, general health — helps AI make judgment calls

### Cooking Preferences
- **Skill level**: beginner / intermediate / advanced
- **Max cooking time**: weeknight vs weekend (e.g., 30min weeknight, 2hr weekend)
- **Batch cooking appetite**: "I'll prep on Sunday for the week" vs "I cook every meal fresh"
- **Equipment available**: oven, hob, microwave, slow cooker, air fryer, instant pot, BBQ, blender, food processor
- **Kitchen notes**: "small kitchen, one hob" — affects recipe complexity

### Budget
- Weekly grocery budget target (£)
- Price sensitivity: "always cheapest" / "mid-range" / "don't care"
- Organic/free-range preference

### Meal Structure
- Which meals per day: breakfast / lunch / dinner / snacks
- Any fixed slots: "I always have overnight oats for weekday breakfast"
- Eating out schedule: "I eat out every Friday dinner"
- Portion size preference: small / normal / large (per meal type)

### Variety & Discovery
- New recipes per week: how many (e.g., 3 new, rest from library)
- Cuisine preferences: ranked list or just liked/disliked
- Adventurousness: "try new things" vs "stick to what I know"
- Seasonal preference: "prefer seasonal/local produce" or don't care

### Shopping
- Primary store: Tesco / Sainsbury's / etc. (affects product matching + pack sizes)
- Shopping frequency: weekly / twice weekly / ad-hoc
- Delivery vs click-and-collect vs in-store

### Onboarding
All of this is a lot to fill in at once. Phased approach:
1. **Essential** (before first plan): dietary identity, allergies, calorie target, household size
2. **Important** (first week): intolerances, dislikes, cooking time limits, budget, meal structure
3. **Nice to have** (learn over time): cuisine preferences, variety settings, equipment, skill level — some of these the AI can infer from feedback
