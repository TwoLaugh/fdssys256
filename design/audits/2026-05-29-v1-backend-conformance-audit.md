- **ai/ai-3** "Structured-output schema validation and corrective re-prompt not wired into dispatch" — claimed HIGH; verifier: The finding's raw evidence is factually accurate, but its characterization as a HIGH MISSING_CAPABILITY defect is wrong — this is deliberately phased, documented deferral, not an unintended gap.

### adaptation/adaptation-2 — Hard-constraint (allergy/dietary) safety-net filter is never invoked inside the worker pipeline [DESIGN_DIVERGENCE · verified-confirmed · triage:FIX]
- **Where:** src/main/java/com/example/mealprep/adaptation/domain/service/AdaptationServiceImpl.java:637-680 (processJob Steps 3 & 6); CandidateGenerator.java:53-63
- **Why:** LLD §Shared worker pipeline is explicit: Step 3 — 'HardConstraintFilterService.filterRecipes drops infeasible candidates BEFORE scoring — never bypassed; the safety net is invariant per HLD §Guardrails'; Step 6 — 'HardConstraintFilterService.checkRecipe re-runs against the FINAL diff — guards against the LLM stitching a candidate together post-hoc.' This mirrors the optimisation-loop HLD's core invariant (optimisation-loop.md lines 75-81): the hard-filter runs before scoring and the LLM 'never touches hard constraints — it picks from a pre-vetted shortlist.' In the implementation, processJob calls candidateGenerator.generate() then scoringEngine.selectTopN() with NO hard-constraint filtering step, and there is NO final-diff re-check after Stage C. - **Fix:** Inject HardConstraintFilterService into the worker; filter candidates before scoring (Step 3/4 boundary) and re-check the final chosen diff before apply (Step 6). On empty-after-filter, fail with HARD_FILTER (already a JobFailureReason).

## Per-domain summaries

**auth** [MINOR_ISSUES]: The auth module is broadly faithful to the LLD: cookie-session auth, hash-only token storage, deny-by-default chain, throttle/lockout state machine, dummy-verify timing parity, and the service-token Pattern-B (origin-tracking) are all implemented and well tested against a real Postgres. The main substantive gaps are a lockout state-machine divergence that re-locks an account on the very first failed attempt after the lockout window expires, an un-wired session reaper (Flow 6), and a password-change path that no longer records failed attempts into the throttle window as the LLD requires. The rest are stale-doc drift (the LLD predates the OpenAPI contract on DTO shapes, username rules, and config prefix) and low-severity smells.

**core** [MINOR_ISSUES]: The shipped core module is internally coherent, cleanly layered, and well-tested — but it was built to its implementation tickets (tickets/core/01, 02a, 02b, 03), which silently redefined large parts of the authoritative LLD (lld/core.md). As a result the LLD is materially stale across ~8 dimensions, and two LLD-mandated v1 capabilities are entirely absent: the X-Trace-Id MDC propagation filter (TraceIdFilter/TraceContext) and the 64 KB decision-log payload budget guard. The origin-tracking pattern (an HLD cross-cutting concern that postdates the LLD) is implemented thoroughly and correctly. No data-loss or security holes; the risks are observability gaps and a design baseline that no longer matches reality.

**recipe** [MINOR_ISSUES]: The recipe *catalogue* (the explicitly in-scope half of the LLD) is implemented thoroughly and conforms closely: versioning, branching, substitutions, revert, promote/demote, scheduled archive, the WriteApi SPI with a FOR-UPDATE race check, events, embeddings, image storage and ratings are all present, and every OpenAPI path maps 1:1 to a controller with swagger-request-validator contract tests. The notable gaps are around *import*: the shared five-layer RecipeExtractionService specified by recipe-extraction-pipeline.md is absent from the recipe module (a simpler 3-strategy HtmlImportParser is used instead, and the real extraction stack lives in the discovery module), recipe deduplication on import/create is an un-implemented TODO, and the Paprika-style preview-then-confirm import flow is not built. Most other deltas are explicitly documented/ticketed divergences rather than defects.

**nutrition** [NEEDS_WORK]: The nutrition module is broad and largely faithful to the LLD for targets, intake CRUD, aggregation, directives, calculation, and the floor gate, with strong test coverage and a complete OpenAPI contract. However several v1 flows the LLD describes as in-scope are shipped as documented stubs: free-text intake override does NOT parse/resolve nutrition (Flow 4), snack logging does NOT resolve via the mapping pipeline nor deduct pantry (Flow 7), and the directive auto-expiry sweep is unimplemented (Flow 8). The floor-gate's hard-floor semantics diverge from the LLD's per-target isHardFloor design, and the LLD-specified getDailyAggregate query + daily-aggregate endpoint are absent.

**planner** [NEEDS_WORK]: The planner's Stage A→B→C→Phase2 deterministic core (beam search, hard filters, seven sub-scores + two gates, rollup, plan persistence, lifecycle state machine, listeners, decision-log chaining) is implemented thoroughly and largely conforms to the LLD. However several design-mandated concurrency and lifecycle guarantees are missing or contradicted: the AI calls run inside the persistence transaction (the opposite of the LLD's locked Tier-1 rule), there is no single-flight generation lock, the weekly PlanCompleted sweep and suggestion-expiry sweep do not exist (so COMPLETED/EXPIRED are unreachable), the cold-start catalogue threshold is computed against distinct slot-kinds instead of slot count, and revert/feasibility/reoptimise diverge materially from the designed contracts. Most gaps are correctness/robustness rather than data-loss, but the transaction-boundary and missing single-flight issues are real v1 risks.

**provisions** [MINOR_ISSUES]: The provisions module is broadly conformant and well-built: inventory CRUD, FIFO-by-expiry cook deduction, grocery import (idempotent + expiry-aware merge), waste logging, planner-bundle read, supplier-product cache, equipment, budget, and per-item audit are all implemented with strong test coverage, and the RFC-9457 error mappings match the LLD table. The notable gaps are deliberately-deferred capabilities the LLD still claims as in-scope: batch-cook splitting is a throwing 422 stub, the pantry-tracking-disable gate is entirely absent, and the PATCH quantity-adjust endpoint was never built. Several class/interface javadocs and the LLD itself are stale relative to the now-fully-built reality (Flow 8 expiry sweep moved to the notification module; cook/grocery event listeners remain dormant no-ops even though the grocery event now exists).

**preference** [NEEDS_WORK]: The taste-profile, lifestyle-config, and preference-archive verticals are well-built and conform closely to the LLD (delta applier, budget guard, version/audit/rollback machinery, section-level audit diffs, and the OpenAPI contract all match what shipped). However the implementation diverges materially from the LLD on the safety-critical hard-constraint filter (no AMBIGUOUS flagging and no dietary-exception context evaluation), and two LLD-scoped v1 capabilities are entirely absent: the Profile Metadata tier (entity/table/endpoints) and the @ValidDietaryIdentity validator. The taste-vector embedding pipeline the LLD presents as locked is also deferred. Several javadocs/comments are stale relative to shipped reality.

**household** [NEEDS_WORK]: The household module is broadly faithful to the LLD — entities, migrations, optimistic locking, the four-table model, the invite lifecycle/status ladder, the last-primary guard, the settings diff/audit, and the full soft-preference merge (which is implemented, not deferred — only the numeric weighting is Out of Scope) all match the design, and the OpenAPI spec is consistent with the shipped controllers.
